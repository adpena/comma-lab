## Core realizations

1. The contest is finite-program synthesis under a scorer. The archive is a
   small deterministic proof object that emits raw bytes favorable to SegNet and
   PoseNet, not a generic video-compression deliverable.
2. CPU and CUDA are separate objective functions for some packets. Device drift
   is not just noise; it is a packet-specific optimization axis that needs
   paired custody.
3. PR106 sidecars are sparse control inputs over the HNeRV latent manifold.
   The next step is better control bases, not arbitrary constants.
4. The current frontier is a local coordinate chart around scorer cliffs. Local
   negatives identify tangent, normal, and threshold-crossing directions.
5. `PacketIR` is the unifying compiler theorem: typed streams, consumed
   sections, byte accounting, identity re-emission, optimize transforms,
   conformance vectors, and fail-closed malformed cases.
6. A serious floor attempt likely needs PR95 training discipline, PR101/103
   packet engineering, and PR106 tiny correction bytes simultaneously.
7. At the PR106 R2 operating point, pose has about `2.78x` the marginal score
   weight of seg, so pose residual atoms are locally high-EV.
8. Non-HNeRV mechanisms should first enter as residual/sidecar programs over
   current champion outputs before full substrate replacement.
9. CMA-ES, Optuna, Bayesian EIG, ADMM, ILP, and branch-and-bound are real only
   when every proposal deterministically materializes charged bytes.
10. A floor proof is a sandwich: scoped lower-bound certificate below and exact
    constructive packet above, with separate CUDA and CPU epsilon gaps.
11. Public PRs are theorem fragments: PR95 training, PR101 schema packing,
    PR103 entropy coding, PR106 CUDA correction, PR97/Quantizr/HPAC non-HNeRV
    grammars.
12. No insight is complete until it changes the build graph.
