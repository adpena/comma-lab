### Family 1: Wyner-Ziv decoder-side-information (Wyner & Ziv 1976)

[Wyner, A. D., & Ziv, J. (1976). "The rate-distortion function for source coding with side information at the decoder." IEEE Transactions on Information Theory.](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/The-rate-distortion-function-for-source-coding-with-Wyner-Ziv/d2607df2b01d1123cae2716b876776510c41f836)

**Mechanism**: Encoder transmits primary signal X given that decoder has side information Y (correlated with X). Rate bound: H(X|Y).

**Sub-variants tested**:
- 1a. `H(cls | gray16)`: 1.145 b/s → **263,868 bytes** (sanity-check matches predecessor's 262,758)
- 1b. `H(cls | gray16, N_cls, W_cls)`: 0.136 b/s → **31,306 bytes** (best Wyner-Ziv variant)

### Family 2: JBIG2 hierarchical context (Howard, Kossentini, Martins, Forchhammer, Rucklidge 1998 / ISO/IEC 14492)

[The emerging JBIG2 standard. Howard et al. 1998. ISO/IEC 14492:2001 / ITU-T T.88.](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/The-emerging-JBIG2-standard-Howard-Kossentini/6dec5ab9f41146ec364c7fc25f03a7fa287cea91)

**Mechanism**: Industry-standard for bi-level / few-class image compression. Uses arithmetic coding with hierarchical 10-pixel context template (MQ coder). Designed specifically for text + halftone + segmentation regions.

**Sub-variant tested**:
- 2a. 6-neighbor template (NN, NW, N, NE, WW, W): 0.215 b/s → **49,584 bytes** (529 active contexts)

**Result**: 27× above T3 budget. JBIG2's hierarchical context is BETTER than predecessor's order-0 (0.215 vs 1.605 b/s) but cannot close the structural gap.

### Family 3: Freeman chain codes (Freeman 1961)

[Freeman, H. (1961). "On the Encoding of Arbitrary Geometric Configurations." IRE Transactions on Electronic Computers.](https://handwiki.org/wiki/Chain_code)

**Mechanism**: Encode only region BOUNDARIES; interior cells are implied by region membership. 8-directional chain code = 3 bits per boundary cell.

**Result**: 43× above T3 budget. Freeman's structural insight is that boundary-cell ratio is 10.95% (most cells are interior). However, the per-region root overhead and boundary-cell density on segmentation maps with 5 classes is still too high for T3.

### Family 4: Quadtree spatial partitioning (Samet 1984)

[Samet, H. (1984). "The Quadtree and Related Hierarchical Data Structures." ACM Computing Surveys.](http://www.cs.umd.edu/~hjs/pubs/SameCSUR84-ocr.pdf)

**Mechanism**: Recursively partition image into 4 quadrants until each leaf is constant-class. Encode tree structure + leaf labels.
