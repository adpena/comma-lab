The doc must include:

1. Mathematical formulation:
   - Pixel-cost map rho(x,y) = gradient-magnitude on SegNet logit map (cheap, deterministic — encoder-time only, NO scorer load at inflate per strict-scorer-rule)
   - Boundary detection threshold: top-5 percent pixels by rho (configurable)
   - STC syndrome: boundary class IDs encoded at H(boundary) bits via Filler/Pevny/Fridrich 2011 STC
   - Non-boundary pixels: encoded as constant-class run-length OR delta from majority class (near-zero cost)
   - Total bytes approximately equal to H(boundary) + tiny non-boundary overhead

   - Do they overlap?
   - Note: STC REPLACES the AV1 monochrome path entirely in mask_codec.py

   - Lane SH is orthogonal (frame count reduction, applies before STC)

5. Boundary detection algorithm:
   - Input: SegNet logits (N, 5, H, W) — but at inflate time we DO NOT have SegNet (strict-scorer-rule)

   - Recommended: encode-time boundary computed from logits via Sobel on argmax-class-ID map (cheap, no scorer needed at decode), then stored as a bit-mask preamble in the syndrome bitstream
   - Decode side reads boundary bitmap, reconstructs cost-map structure, decodes STC syndrome
