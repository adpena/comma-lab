## L3 absence ‚Äî explanation

Zero lanes are at Level 3 in the current registry. L3 requires all 7 gates:
`impl_complete` + `real_archive_empirical` + `contest_cuda` + `contest_cpu`
+ `strict_preflight` + `three_clean_review` + `memory_entry` + `deploy_runbook`.
The dual-CUDA-CPU gate (`contest_cuda` AND `contest_cpu`) is the bottleneck:
per the CLAUDE.md "Submission auth eval ‚Äî BOTH CPU AND CUDA" non-negotiable
landed 2026-05-08, even `lane_g_v3` (the original L3 anchor at 1.05
[contest-CUDA] T4) is now downgraded to L2 because its `[contest-CPU]` Linux
x86_64 anchor was never captured.

This is the correct posture for v0.2.0-rc1: NO lane is "fully production
hardened" against the public-leaderboard ranking axis until both axes are
captured on 1:1 contest-CI hardware. L2 means "integrated with empirical
anchor on at least one axis"; L3 means "production-hardened on BOTH axes."

The v0.2.0-rc1 release manifest reflects this honestly: every L2 lane carries
an axis-tagged score; no lane is "promoted" to "frontier" without dual-axis
evidence; the GHA-CPU-eval queue infrastructure landed 2026-05-12 (commit
`62e976d2`) is the canonical mechanism for filling the `[contest-CPU]` gap.
