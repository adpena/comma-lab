- **Source:** C6 v1 architecture (`MDLIBPSConfig.latent_dim=24`, continuous Gaussian `q(z|frames) = N(μ, σ²)`, KL regularizer to N(0, I) prior).

The empirical SegNet=2.60 (86% of total) IS structurally consistent with this prediction.
- **Scientific axis:** Hafner 2024 DreamerV3 paper documents 5-10× sample-efficiency improvement when replacing continuous-Gaussian posterior with CATEGORICAL posterior (24 groups × K=256 categories = 192 bits per sample, ~4× higher capacity at the same latent dimensionality). Higgins 2017 β-VAE empirically showed β ∈ [1e-5, 1e-4] often optimal for dense-output tasks (3 orders of magnitude below C6's β=0.01).
- **Engineering axis:** Sister F=Z8 (canonical quadruple LANDED) uses HIERARCHICAL IB binding per Rao-Ballard 1999 + Mallat wavelet multi-scale — explicit FORK from C6 v1's scalar continuous-Gaussian posterior. Sister A=DreamerV3 RSSM (LANDED) uses CATEGORICAL posterior at 32×32 = 1024 categories per latent group. Both sisters operationalize alternative reducer methodologies; neither has post-training Tier-C anchor yet.
- **Classification:** **CARGO-CULTED-PENDING-EMPIRICAL** (HARD-EARNED at math + scientific axes; engineering axis offers ≥2 alternative reducer methodologies that have NOT been empirically anchored on the dashcam scorer).
- **UNWIND DISPOSITION FOR J:** J=MDL-IBPS MUST distinguish from sister F=Z8 (hierarchical binding) and sister A=DreamerV3 RSSM (categorical posterior). ### CC-J-4 [MEDIUM PRIORITY]: "Variational KL upper bound is tight" — CARGO-CULTED-LOOSE-BOUND

- **Source:** C6 v1 `mdl_loss.py` uses `KL(q(z|frames) || p(z))` as upper bound on `I(z; frames)`.
- **Math axis:** Tishby-Zaslavsky 2017 VIB derivation: `I(z; frames) ≤ KL(q(z|x) || p(z))` is the variational upper bound; equality holds when q(z|x) IS the true posterior. In practice for high-dim x (frames), q(z|x) is parameterized by a small MLP/CNN and the bound is LOOSE (gap can be ~10× depending on encoder capacity).
- **Scientific axis:** Belghazi et al. 2018 MINE (Mutual Information Neural Estimation) provides a TIGHT lower bound on I(z; frames) via Donsker-Varadhan dual representation. Poole et al. 2019 "On Variational Bounds of Mutual Information" enumerates the tight-vs-loose tradeoffs across the InfoNCE / NWJ / DV / TUBA family.
- **Engineering axis:** Sister substrate Z3-G1 in `experiments/train_substrate_z3_g1_scorer_softmax_hyperprior_gating.py` uses hyperprior + scorer-softmax — orthogonal approach to MI estimation. No sister substrate has yet operationalized MINE.
- **Classification:** **CARGO-CULTED-METHODOLOGY-DEFAULT** (HARD-EARNED at math axis per Tishby-Zaslavsky 2017; CARGO-CULTED at scientific axis because tighter MI estimators exist; CARGO-CULTED at engineering axis because no sister has tested MINE).
- **UNWIND DISPOSITION FOR J:** J's Phase 2 substrate-design decision may consider MINE-based MI estimation as an UNIQUE FORK of the canonical IB loss; if MINE is adopted, design memo MUST cite empirical evidence the looseness of variational KL is a measurable drag on the substrate's score; if NOT adopted, design memo MUST cite the engineering-simplicity tradeoff.
