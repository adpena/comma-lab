## 5. The 1-ch vs 3-ch crash — root cause hypothesis

Lane V crashed at: `weight of size [32, 3, 3, 3], expected input[1, 1, 384, 512] to have 3 channels, but got 1` (memory: `project_lane_v_crashed_channel_mismatch_20260428`).

Most likely culprit (NOT the half-frame logic itself): the `[32, 3, 3, 3]` weight is consistent with the renderer's first conv expecting a **3-channel `embedding(mask)` output** (embed_dim=6 ÷ 2 reshape, or pixel-RGB-like input). The training pipeline at `mask_half_sim_prob=1.0` may be passing through a code path where mask is fed as 1-channel raw uint8 instead of through `nn.Embedding` first. **But this is downstream of the bigger problem**: even if we fix the channel bug, the resulting model is still NOT what Quantizr built.

## 6. Verdict + recommendation

**Lane V (current) is NOT a faithful replica.** It's a "Quantizr-inspired" lane that kept our existing warp-based architecture and bolted on:
- depthwise-separable convs ✓ (matches Quantizr)
- 88K param target ✓ (matches numerically)
- FiLM pose conditioning ✓ (we have `pose_dim`)
- mask_half_sim_prob=1.0 ✗ (Quantizr doesn't simulate-warp during training; he just trains with single-mask input from epoch 0)
- KL distill T=2.0 ✓ (matches)

But it kept the **motion module + warp + dual-mask** structure that Quantizr explicitly removed.

**Recommendation: Do NOT redeploy Lane V or its V2/annealed variant.** Instead:

1. **Build a new lane (Lane V-CLEAN or Lane Q-FAITHFUL)** that copies Quantizr's `JointFrameGenerator` architecture verbatim:
   - `SharedMaskDecoder(num_classes=5, emb_dim=6, c1=56, c2=64, depth_mult=1)` (single-mask trunk)
   - `pose_mlp = Linear(6,48) → SiLU → Linear(48,48)` (FiLM conditioning)
   - `Frame2StaticHead` for frame2 (no FiLM)
   - `FrameHead` with `FiLMSepResBlock` for frame1 (FiLM on pose)
   - **No MotionPredictor, no warp_with_flow, no ego_flow, no RadialZoomWarp dependency.**
2. **New inflate path** (or new branch in `inflate_renderer.py`): single-mask, single-pose, two-output renderer. The current half-frame inflate path warps odd→even — for Quantizr's design, that is **wrong** and unnecessary.
3. **Archive layout**: `renderer.bin` (FP4-packed) + `mask.mkv` (600 odd-frame masks) + `poses.pt` (`[600, 6]` float32, brotli-compressed). No `zoom_scalars.pt`.
4. **Cost estimate**: ~4-6h of new architecture wiring + tests, then a fresh ~12h training run on 4090 ($3-4). Total ~$5-6 to land a true 88K-Quantizr replica.

**Why "half-frame was killed when we had the knowledge that it works all along"**: We *did* know half-frame works (Quantizr 0.33 proves it). What we built was a half-frame *retrofit* of our warp-based renderer, which is a different thing. The Lane V crash + Lane D's earlier `feedback_half_frame_breaks_posenet` failure both stem from gluing half-frame onto a renderer that was never designed for it. **Quantizr's 0.33 doesn't come from half-frame alone — it comes from a single-mask + FiLM-on-pose architecture that makes half-frame trivial to encode.**

## 7. Strategic note

The user's instinct ("our quantizr reverse engineer is bugged and broken and maybe doesn't use half-warp and isn't engineered correctly") was correct. The replica diverges from the source at the architectural-family level, not at the hyperparameter level. Param-count matching is a red herring; the structure matters.

The good news: **Quantizr's actual architecture is SIMPLER than ours** (no motion module, no warp, no ego_flow plumbing). A faithful rebuild is mostly subtraction, not addition. Estimated effort to land a working clone: 1 day of design + code + tests; ~$5 of GPU. If it works, it directly enables the [0.30, 0.50] Stack C moonshot the original Lane V was supposed to anchor.
