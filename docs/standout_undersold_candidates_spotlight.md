The inventory enumerates eleven paradigm classes evenly; this memo is the 90-second skim a reader should reach for first.

The submission packet for [`commaai/comma_video_compression_challenge#110`](https://github.com/commaai/comma_video_compression_challenge/pull/110) is intentionally narrow. Most of the apparatus that produced it lives outside the packet. The ten candidates below are the most substantive of what lives outside.

## A. Why this memo exists

PR #110's body is calibrated and short on purpose. The inventory memo gives every paradigm class equal billing because that is honest accounting: most of them are SCAFFOLDED or DESIGN-ONLY, and ranking them by "promise" before empirical anchors land would be exactly the design-time band-claim failure mode (the C6 IBPS 22× miss anchor in the inventory) the apparatus exists to prevent.

But equal billing has a cost: a reader who only has 90 seconds cannot tell which of the eleven classes is worth opening first. This memo is the targeted answer to "if I read three things about comma-lab beyond the submission, which three?" The criterion is not "predicted-best score" — every prediction is uncertain at the 0.001–0.01 scale the inventory acknowledges. The criterion is "promising by canonical-reference fit to the dashcam-video + scorer-as-receiver problem, and substantively built out enough that the next step is well-defined."

Empirical-vs-design state is preserved honestly per candidate. Score literals carry axis + hardware tags. Where a candidate has been falsified at a specific implementation it is called out as implementation-level, not paradigm-level, with the canonical reactivation criterion.

## B. Standout candidates

### B.1 Z7-Mamba-2 — selective state-space predictive coding

A Mamba-2 selective state-space recurrent network (Dao & Gu 2024) acting as the temporal-prediction substrate in a Rao-Ballard hierarchical predictive-coding stack. The contest video is twenty seconds of ego-motion dashcam footage; the next-frame prior is dominated by translational and rotational optical flow, which structured state-space models with selective scan are designed to capture more efficiently than the transformer baseline. The substrate exposes a canonical `forward(z_prev, ego_motion) -> z_t` signature with a `stateful=True` recurrent mode (Wyner-Ziv implicit side-information channel) and a `stateful=False` ablation control. Two backends ship: production CUDA kernel via the upstream `mamba_ssm` package, and a pure-PyTorch reference for MPS / CPU local development. Current state: SCAFFOLDED with the canonical helper at `src/tac/optimization/mamba2_predictor.py`, sister-substrate at `src/tac/substrates/time_traveler_l5_z6/architecture.py`, design memo + parent symposium adjudicated, MPS reference path under active iteration. Next milestone is a 100ep canary smoke on Modal A100 once the reference-torch handoff stabilizes (`$15`–`$30`, ~6h wall-clock). Canonical references: Dao & Gu 2024 [arXiv:2405.21060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.21060); Rao & Ballard 1999 [Nature Neuroscience 2(1):79–87](https://www.nature.com/articles/nn0199_79); Hafner et al. 2023 DreamerV3 [arXiv:2301.04104](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04104).

### B.2 RAFT-derived poses + LAPose codec

A calibrated 6-DoF pose decomposition derived from RAFT (Teed & Deng 2020) optical-flow fields, plus a dedicated pose-axis codec that allocates bits non-uniformly across the focus-of-expansion region under forward motion (Gibson's 1950 ego-motion + FOE framing). The math is clean: under forward motion the normalized-coordinate flow field decomposes into translation flow (depth-dependent, radial from FOE) plus rotation flow (depth-independent, polynomial in image coordinates); fitting six basis coefficients per frame via least-squares pseudoinverse recovers a 6-DoF pose initialization that the existing pose-TTO loop then refines. Two operating modes: compress-time-only as a pose prior (safe for contest submission) and inflate-time recompute (eliminates ~50 KB of shipped pose bytes, but requires explicit operator approval per the strict-scorer rule). LAPose is the pose-axis dedicated codec that consumes the RAFT-derived signal. Files: `src/tac/raft_radial_pose.py`, `src/tac/raft_pose.py` (the single-DOF predecessor), `src/tac/lapose_motion_atom_allocator.py`, `src/tac/codec_pipeline_kl_pose.py`. Current state: SCAFFOLDED with a prior Lane-FL empirical dispatch on Vast.ai 4090 anchoring the single-DOF case; full 6-DoF Mode A is the next-step dispatch. Canonical references: Teed & Deng 2020 [arXiv:2003.12039](https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.12039); Longuet-Higgins 1981 [Nature 293:133–135](https://www.nature.com/articles/293133a0); Mallat 2009 [*A Wavelet Tour of Signal Processing*, 3rd ed.](https://www.elsevier.com/books/a-wavelet-tour-of-signal-processing/mallat/978-0-12-374370-1) Ch. 6.

### B.3 CLADE — class-adaptive denormalization

Per-class affine lookup conditioning for the renderer, using the segmentation mask as the class-label source. The contest scorer's segmentation distortion term reads off the SegNet's per-class predictions on the rendered frame; conditioning the renderer's normalization layers on per-class affine parameters lets the renderer learn class-specific texture priors (road texture for the road class, sky gradient for the sky class, vehicle silhouette for the vehicle class) without paying for the full spatial-resolution lookup that SPADE requires. The compute cost is roughly an order of magnitude smaller than SPADE because the conditioning is a class-index lookup rather than a learned conv-net evaluation over the segmentation mask. Files: `src/tac/categorical_candidate_readiness.py`, `src/tac/semantic_quantization.py`, `src/tac/categorical_openpilot_mask_prior_contract.py`, `src/tac/categorical_payload_candidate.py`, and the renderer that consumes both — `src/tac/dp_sims_renderer.py`. Current state: SCAFFOLDED as a paired alternative to SPADE; the dp_sims_renderer currently uses SPADE by default, with CLADE as the lower-compute swap. Canonical reference: Tan, Chen, Wang & Wei 2021 [arXiv:2012.04644](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.04644). Next milestone is a paired-architecture smoke comparing CLADE-conditioned vs SPADE-conditioned renderer at matched compute budget (~$5 each on T4).

### B.4 SPADE — spatially-adaptive denormalization

The full spatially-varying-conditioning sister to CLADE. SPADE learns spatially-varying gamma and beta parameters from the segmentation mask via a small convolutional network at every renderer layer, so each pixel's normalization adapts to its local class context rather than its global class membership. This is more expressive than CLADE — the renderer can learn intra-class spatial patterns (a road far from the camera looks different from a road near the camera even though both pixels belong to the road class) — at the cost of running the SPADE conv network at every conditioning point. SPADE is the canonical semantic-conditioned synthesis baseline from CVPR 2019 (NVIDIA GauGAN); the dashcam-video context is a natural fit because the segmentation mask is precisely the conditioning signal the contest scorer rewards faithful preservation of. Files: same as CLADE plus `src/tac/dp_sims_renderer.py` which currently ships SPADE as the default conditioning. Current state: SCAFFOLDED end-to-end inside the dp_sims_renderer with multi-scale PatchGAN discriminator option and cross-attention noise injection for texture diversity. Canonical reference: Park, Liu, Wang & Zhu 2019 [arXiv:1903.07291](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.07291). Next milestone is the paired CLADE vs SPADE compute-vs-quality study (the comparison is the question, not which one wins).

### B.5 SIREN — sinusoidal coordinate MLP

A coordinate-MLP with sinusoidal activations whose network weights ARE the compressed video. SIREN's sine activations capture high-frequency detail that ReLU coordinate-MLPs cannot represent at small parameter counts — for image and video INR (implicit neural representation) tasks, SIREN routinely matches reconstruction quality at one-tenth the parameter count. The relevance to the contest is direct: HNeRV (the substrate the medal cluster builds on) is itself a hybrid INR, and SIREN provides a class-shift candidate at the coordinate-MLP corner of the INR design space. The target size for a SIREN video memorization of the contest's 20-second clip is 50–100KB total network weights — smaller than the current HNeRV-family archive cluster. Files: the codec primitive at `src/tac/network_codec.py` (`SIRENLayer`, `SIRENVideoCodec`), the profile entry in `src/tac/profiles.py` (`NETWORK_CODEC_SMOKE`, `NETWORK_CODEC_FULL`), and the composition registration in `src/tac/optimization/substrate_composition_matrix.py`. Current state: SCAFFOLDED with a prior smoke dispatch where the trainer timed out at 1h on T4; the reactivation criterion is a longer-budget dispatch on 4090 with the post-mortem fix (the byte-identical renderer.bin issue that the no-op detector caught was an implementation-level falsification, not a paradigm falsification). Canonical reference: Sitzmann, Martel, Bergman, Lindell & Wetzstein 2020 NeurIPS [arXiv:2006.09661](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.09661).

### B.6 UNIWARD — texture-aware distortion cost

The Holub-Fridrich-Denemark 2014 universal distortion function for steganography, applied as the distortion-cost lens for compress-time byte allocation. The intuition is direct: errors injected into textured regions of an image are undetectable to a CNN-class steganalysis detector; errors injected into smooth regions are detectable. The contest scorer is, per the lineage Yousfi established at Binghamton's DDE Lab, structurally an inverse-steganalysis primitive — a CNN trained to flag pixel-level perturbation. So weighting the codec's bit-allocation loss by inverse local wavelet-variance (the UNIWARD cost map) directs bits AWAY from the smooth regions where the scorer would penalize errors and TOWARD the textured regions where it is blind. Implementation-wise this lands as a sparse, L∞-bounded delta-injection on rendered frames before the camera-resolution upscale, optimized at compress-time against the actual PoseNet (and optional SegNet) loss; at inflate-time the delta is a pure additive lookup table with no scorer load (strict-scorer-rule compliant). Files: `src/tac/uniward_delta.py`, `src/tac/wavelet_variance.py`, `src/tac/saliency_inversion.py`, `src/tac/score_gradient_param_saliency.py`. Canonical references: Holub, Fridrich & Denemark 2014 [EURASIP JIS 2014(1):1](https://jis-eurasipjournals.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/1687-417X-2014-1); Fridrich & Kodovský 2012 [IEEE TIFS 7(3):868–882](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/6197267). Next milestone is integration with the per-pair master-gradient bit-allocator (B.8 below) so the UNIWARD cost map gates which pixels the per-pair budget can spend on.

### B.7 Compress-time freezing + gradient-stop toolkit

A canonical package of eight focused freezing-exploit helpers — each under 200 LOC — for surgical parameter-subset training. The pattern: train the renderer for N warmstart epochs with PoseNet receiving gradient (so the renderer learns to satisfy the score-aware loss), then freeze PoseNet's gradient flow while continuing renderer training. This makes the back half of training cheaper without degrading score-aware quality. The package channels patterns from eight canonical sources: Karpathy nanoGPT (minimal precise freeze patterns), Hugging Face PEFT (LoRA frozen-base + trainable-low-rank-adapter), the lottery-ticket hypothesis (Frankle & Carbin 2019 magnitude-prune + freeze), SWA (Izmailov et al. 2018 stochastic weight averaging), and the canonical EMA freeze-at-eval snapshot+restore that every trainer in this repo must honor. The Hinton 2014 KL-on-logits T=2.0 frozen-teacher distillation primitive is the canonical entry point for the bolt-on-on-HNeRV-substrate pattern. Files: `src/tac/freezing/__init__.py` (canonical package surface), `src/tac/freezing/pose_gradient_stop_after_warmstart.py`, `src/tac/freezing/lora_style_renderer_adapter.py`, `src/tac/freezing/frozen_teacher_distillation.py`, `src/tac/freezing/swa_checkpoint_averaging.py`, `src/tac/freezing/lottery_ticket_extraction.py`, `src/tac/freezing/ema_freeze_at_eval.py`, `src/tac/training_curriculum/__init__.py`, plus working integration in `src/tac/substrates/a1_plus_wavelet_residual/score_aware_loss.py`. Current state: LANDED as a reusable toolkit with focused tests; consumed by multiple substrates. Canonical references: Frankle, Schwab & Saxe 2020 [arXiv:2003.00152](https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.00152); Hinton, Vinyals & Dean 2015 [arXiv:1503.02531](https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.02531); Hu et al. 2021 LoRA [arXiv:2106.09685](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685); Izmailov et al. 2018 SWA [arXiv:1803.05407](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05407).

### B.8 Master-gradient extractor + per-pair Lagrangian planner

A diagnostic surface that decomposes the additive scorer `S = 100·d_seg + sqrt(10·d_pose) + 25·R` across the parser-known payload domain at zero GPU cost (the extractor runs on CPU against a landed archive). The output is a per-pair, per-byte sensitivity map that downstream consumers — the cathedral autopilot ranker, the per-pair Pareto envelope, the bit allocator — query to make substrate-fit decisions before paid GPU spend. The math is canonical: a Taylor expansion plus Cauchy-Schwarz bound gives a per-pair upper bound on score-impact that the per-pair-residual-rate of any candidate substrate must beat to be competitive. The Lagrangian-dual per-pair treatment plan is the consumer that ingests the sensitivity map and produces per-pair recommendations (which pairs get the budget; which substrate primitive each pair routes to). Authority-boundary discipline is strict: the master gradient is a diagnostic surface, not a promotion authority — every score-impact claim must route through `CandidateModificationSpec` with grammar-aware operator coordinates and packet proofs. Files: `src/tac/master_gradient.py`, `src/tac/master_gradient_consumers.py`, `tools/extract_master_gradient.py`, `tools/master_gradient_xray.py`, plus the cathedral consumer at `src/tac/master_gradient_per_pair_difficulty_atlas_consumer/`. Current state: LANDED with active integration across multiple cathedral consumers and downstream allocators. Canonical references: Cauchy-Schwarz inequality (canonical); Boyd & Vandenberghe 2004 [*Convex Optimization*, Cambridge UP](https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/cvxbook/) for Lagrangian duality; Cover & Thomas 2006 [*Elements of Information Theory*, 2nd ed., Wiley](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Elements+of+Information+Theory%2C+2nd+Edition-p-9780471241959) Ch. 2 for the additive Shannon framework.

### B.9 Cargo-cult-unwind methodology + canonical equations registry

A meta-engineering discipline: classify every substrate-design assumption as either HARD-EARNED (cite the canonical reference + the empirical anchor that validated the transfer to the dashcam-video context) or CARGO-CULTED (inherited from the canonical reference without testing whether it transfers). For each CARGO-CULTED assumption, design an unwind probe. The canonical empirical anchor is NSCS06 v6 → v7: 105.15 → 58.89 contest-CUDA in ONE iteration after unwinding 4 of 7 cargo-culted assumptions (a 44% reduction). The methodology operationalizes the standing "no premature kill without research exhaustion" non-negotiable — implementation-level falsification at v6 was not paradigm-level kill; the paradigm was intact, the implementation carried inherited assumptions that needed empirical surfacing. The canonical equations registry is the sister artifact: an fcntl-locked append-only JSONL ledger of empirically-calibrated equations (e.g. `brotli_cascade_bounded_per_stream_v1`, `mps_drift_architecture_class_dependent_v1`, `per_pair_master_gradient_score_impact_taylor_v1`) with Bayesian posterior updating against landed anchors. Together they extinct tribal knowledge: every quantitative belief the apparatus holds is a row in the registry, not a docstring claim. Files: the methodology lives in the recursive-adversarial-review protocol + Assumption-Adversary council seat documented in this repo's contributor standards; the canonical equations registry is at `src/tac/canonical_equations/`. Canonical references: Wang & Rudin 2015 ["Falling Rule Lists"](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v38/wang15a.html) AISTATS for the assumption-classification lineage; the meta-engineering pattern itself is operator-pioneered. Companion memo: [cargo-cult unwind methodology](./cargo_cult_unwind_methodology.md).

### B.10 4-tier council discipline + assumption-adversary seat

A meta-engineering rigor layer: a 4-tier council hierarchy with explicit per-tier quorum, tie-break, and operator-attention budgets, plus a dedicated Assumption-Adversary seat whose mandate per round is to challenge the SHARED ASSUMPTIONS framing the discussion rather than the arguments within them. The shared-leadership core is four co-leads — Shannon (information-theory grounding), Dykstra (alternating-projections feasibility), Rudin (interpretable-ML / falling-rule-list / GOSDT decision-path), Daubechies (wavelets / multi-scale partition prior) — that together cover the four orthogonal axes the meta-Lagrangian/Pareto solver depends on. Per-tier discipline forces decisions to land at the right cadence (T1 unbounded working groups, T2 inner skunkworks at ≤3/day, T3 full grand council at ≤3/week, T4 symposium at ≤2/month) so that procedural rigor does not displace mission velocity. Each council deliberation emits a canonical posterior anchor (fcntl-locked JSONL append) with explicit per-member assumption surfacing, dissent verbatim preservation, and HARD-EARNED-vs-CARGO-CULTED classification — making every council deliberation queryable across sessions. The downstream consumers (cathedral autopilot ranker, Rashomon ensemble, future deliberations' cite-chains) read the posterior directly. Per the standing "mission alignment" rule, the discipline serves the mission, not the reverse: operator-frontier-override is the documented escape hatch at every tier when a frontier-breaking move cannot wait for tier-required quorum. This is not a contest-score-directly contribution but it multiplies every substrate-design decision's quality and is the rigor layer that lets the rest of the apparatus operate at speed.

## C. How these compose

The ten candidates are not independent. They compose as a coherent toolkit:

- The **master-gradient extractor (B.8)** feeds substrate-fit diagnostic for everything below at zero GPU cost. Every per-pair / per-byte sensitivity claim downstream of it inherits the canonical Taylor + Cauchy-Schwarz bound.
- **Z7-Mamba-2 (B.1)** and **RAFT-derived poses (B.2)** compose as a temporal-prediction codec stack: the Mamba-2 selective scan captures the latent-dynamics part of the next-frame prior; the RAFT-derived pose stream captures the ego-motion conditioning that the Mamba-2 substrate consumes.
- **CLADE (B.3)** and **SPADE (B.4)** provide the semantic conditioning that Z7-Mamba-2's spatial decoder uses to render a frame from its predicted latent; the CLADE vs SPADE choice is a compute-vs-quality tradeoff the dp_sims_renderer makes per-deployment.
- **SIREN (B.5)** provides the coordinate-MLP primitive that the spatial decoder of any INR-class substrate (HNeRV, Z7-Mamba-2's decoder, the dp_sims_renderer's upsampler) can drop in as a sister to convolutional decoders.
- **UNIWARD (B.6)** provides the cost-function lens that turns the score-axis insensitivity signal (per-pixel) into a bit-allocation signal — directing the per-pair budget that B.8 produces toward the textured regions where the scorer is blind.
- **Freezing + gradient-stop (B.7)** provides the surgical parameter-subset training primitive that any of the above substrates can use to make their training cheaper without degrading the score-aware quality.
- **Cargo-cult-unwind (B.9)** and **council discipline (B.10)** are the meta-engineering layer that operates over all of the above: every substrate's design memo is audited for HARD-EARNED-vs-CARGO-CULTED assumptions before paid dispatch, and every dispatch verdict is recorded as canonical posterior the next iteration can cite.

Read this composition graph as "where these go next" rather than "what they currently do together." Most of the cross-substrate composition is design-only at this writing; the inventory in the sister memo is honest about which pairs have been empirically anchored. The composition graph is the next-direction map.
